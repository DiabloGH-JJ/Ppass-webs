Mole National Park

Mole National Park, Ghana’s largest wildlife refuge, spans the Northern Region and was established in 1958. It’s famous for its elephant safaris and vast savannah landscapes, hosting a rich diversity of fauna.

Main Details

  • Size: 4840 km²
  • Ecosystem: Guinea Savannah
  • Wildlife Highlights: Elephants, lions, leopards, and over 300 bird species
  • Famous For: Elephant safaris and observation points

Wildlife Highlights

  • Elephants (large herds)
  • Lions, leopards, and hyenas
  • Antelopes and baboons
  • Over 300 bird species including vultures

Activities

  • Guided safaris and walking tours
  • Birdwatching from observation points
  • Camping and night drives
  • Photography tours

Fun Facts

  • It’s Ghana’s largest national park at 4,840 km²!
  • Home to over 90 mammal species.
  • Established in 1958, it’s the oldest park in Ghana.
