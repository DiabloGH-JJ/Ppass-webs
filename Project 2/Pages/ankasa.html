Ankasa Park

Ankasa Park, in Ghana’s Western Region, is a biodiversity hotspot established in 1976. It’s celebrated for its rich variety of plants, butterflies, and bird species within a dense rainforest.

Main Details

  • Size: 509 km²
  • Ecosystem: Tropical Rainforest
  • Wildlife Highlights: Monkeys, birds, and numerous butterfly species
  • Famous For: Butterfly and bird diversity

Wildlife Highlights

  • Various monkey species
  • Over 200 bird species
  • 600+ butterfly species
  • Diverse plant life

Activities

  • Guided forest walks
  • Birdwatching and butterfly tours
  • Plant identification tours
  • Photography expeditions
  • It’s one of Ghana’s wettest parks with over 1,800 mm of rain annually!
  • Home to the rare bongo antelope.
  • Established in 1976 as a conservation area.
