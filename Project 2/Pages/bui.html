Bui National Park

Bui National Park, located in Ghana’s Brong-Ahafo Region, is a scenic reserve established in 1971 along the Black Volta River. It’s renowned for its hippo-filled river cruises and stunning escarpments, offering a blend of aquatic and terrestrial biodiversity.

Main Details

  • Size: 1821 km²
  • Ecosystem: Savannah and Riverine Forest
  • Wildlife Highlights: Hippos, crocodiles, antelopes, and various bird species
  • Famous For: River cruises and the Bui Dam

Wildlife Highlights

  • Hippos (significant population along the Black Volta)
  • Crocodiles and water chevrotain
  • Antelopes and buffalo
  • Over 200 bird species including fish eagles

Activities

  • River cruises and boat tours
  • Game viewing and birdwatching
  • Fishing (with permits)
  • Hiking along escarpments
