Bia Game Production Reserve

Bia Game Production Reserve, in Ghana’s Western Region, is a pristine rainforest established in 1974. It’s known for its diverse wildlife and hiking opportunities within its lush greenery.

Main Details

  • Size: 306 km²
  • Ecosystem: Tropical Rainforest
  • Wildlife Highlights: Bongo antelopes, monkeys, and forest hogs
  • Famous For: Biodiversity and hiking trails

Wildlife Highlights

  • Bongo antelopes and forest hogs
  • Various monkey species
  • Over 200 bird species
  • Rich insect and plant diversity

Activities

  • Guided hiking tours
  • Wildlife observation
  • Birdwatching
  • Research and educational tours

Fun Facts

  • It has some of West Africa’s tallest trees!
  • Named after the Bia River, it became a park in 1974.
  • Designated a UNESCO biosphere reserve in 1985.
