Kakum National Park

Located in Ghana’s Central Region, Kakum National Park is a lush tropical rainforest reserve established in 1992. Originally a forest reserve in 1931, it gained fame for its iconic Canopy Walkway—a 350-meter-long suspended bridge system connecting treetops, giving visitors a unique aerial view of the forest ecosystem.

Main Details

  • Size: 375 km²
  • Ecosystem: Dense Tropical Rainforest
  • Wildlife Highlights: Forest elephants, Diana monkeys, Bongo antelope, and over 300 bird species
  • Famous For: The Canopy Walkway—a 350-meter-long suspended bridge system connecting 7 treetops, offering a bird's-eye view of the forest

Wildlife Highlights

  • Forest elephants (densest population in Ghana)
  • Diana monkeys, yellow-backed duikers, Bongo antelope
  • Over 300 bird species including hornbills and the endangered white-breasted guineafowl
  • 600+ butterfly species, with a new species discovered in 1993

Activities

  • Guided forest walks (day and night)
  • Medicinal plant tours
  • Birdwatching and butterfly spotting
  • Cultural performances and treehouse overnight stays

Fun Facts

  • It’s one of only three places in Africa with a canopy walkway!
  • A new butterfly species was discovered here in 1993.
  • The park was created through local initiative, not the state wildlife department.
