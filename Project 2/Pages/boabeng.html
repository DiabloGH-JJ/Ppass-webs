Boabeng Monkey Sanctuary

Boabeng Monkey Sanctuary, in Ghana’s Ashanti Region, is a sacred forest reserve established to protect its monkey population. It’s known for its human-like monkeys and cultural significance.

Main Details

  • Size: 19 km²
  • Ecosystem: Dry Semi-Deciduous Forest
  • Wildlife Highlights: Mona monkeys, black-and-white colobus monkeys
  • Famous For: Monkey watching and cultural heritage

Wildlife Highlights

  • Mona monkeys and colobus monkeys
  • Small antelope species
  • Various bird species
  • Rich tree diversity

Activities

  • Monkey watching tours
  • Cultural performances
  • Forest walks
  • Photography

Fun Facts

  • Locals consider the monkeys sacred and protect them!
  • It spans just 4.7 km² but is rich in biodiversity.
  • Monkeys are buried with ceremonies when they die.
